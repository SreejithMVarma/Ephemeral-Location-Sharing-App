# Ephemeral Radar: What's Working Now

Here is a simple breakdown of what you can currently do in the app:

### 🎯 Creating & Joining Radars
* **Create a Radar:** Start a private, temporary location-sharing session (we call it a "Radar").
* **Share via QR Code & Links:** Easily invite friends by letting them scan a QR code on your screen or by sending them a direct link.
* **Join a Radar:** Hop into a session by scanning a friend's QR code or clicking their invite link.
* **Waiting Room:** See who has joined before officially jumping into the live map.

### 📍 Live Location Tracking
* **Real-Time Updates:** As you and your friends move, everyone's position updates live on the Radar screen.
* **Distance & Direction:** The app calculates exactly how far away your friends are and points you in their direction.
* **Smart Battery Usage:** The app keeps sharing your location seamlessly in the background as long as you're in an active Radar session, even if your phone is locked. However, to save your battery and data, it only downloads and computes *other* people's live locations when you actually have the Radar screen open on your phone.

### 🛡️ Privacy & Controls
* **Choose Your Visibility:** You have total control over what others see. You can show just your general direction, your direction + exact distance, or your precise spot on a full map.
* **Host Powers:** If you created the Radar, you can kick out unwanted guests or delete the entire session for everyone.
* **Session History:** The app remembers Radars you've joined recently so you can quickly hop back in without needing the invite link again.

### 🛠️ Behind the Scenes
* **Ephemeral by Design:** All location data is temporary. When a session ends, the data disappears.
* **Live WebSocket Connection:** The app uses a fast, persistent connection to make sure location blips move smoothly and instantly without needing to refresh.
